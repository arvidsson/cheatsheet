Numbers

length(a) = \( |a| \)

distance(a,b) = \( |b - a| \)

sign(a) = \( a / |a| \)

Vectors

length(v) = \( ||v|| = \sqrt{v.x^2 + v.y^2} \)

distance(a,b) = \( ||b - a|| \)

unit vector = normalized vector = length of 1

direction(v) = \( a / ||a|| \)

When \(a \ne 0\), there are two solutions to \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\) and they are \[x = {-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac} \over 2a}.\]
